# World Lore — Nocturne

## Setting

Dark industrial steampunk city. Dense urban architecture — gaslit streets, steam pipes, iron scaffolding, cramped tenements pressed up against grand private mansions. The aesthetic is Victorian-industrial: brass fittings, aged stone, narrow alleys, perpetual night-shift workers and curfew-enforced silence after dark.

The world is authoritarian. Citizens do not go out after curfew. Guards patrol for the state. Wealth and rank are legible through GameplayTags — guards react to the role of whoever stands before them, not the face.

---

## The Purge (100 Years Ago)

A century ago, a dictator rose to power and ordered the systematic execution of all people with supernatural abilities ("gifted" people). The purge was thorough:

- Gifted people were hunted, killed, or driven underground
- Public knowledge of gifted abilities was suppressed and erased
- The current generation — including our heroes — **has no awareness that gifted people ever existed**
- The world treats abilities as myth or superstition, if it thinks of them at all

The dictator's regime has since stabilized into a permanent authoritarian order. The purge is forgotten history, not living fear.

---

## The Survivors

A small number of gifted people survived by hiding. Their descendants still carry these abilities. They are rare enough that:

- The state no longer actively hunts them (they are forgotten, not outlawed)
- Even the survivors' descendants may not fully grasp the historical weight of what they can do
- Discovery by the wrong person would still be dangerous — but no systematic hunt is underway

---

## Our Heroes

Two thieves-for-hire. Survivors' descendants. They work the same city, take the same jobs, and don't lead with their abilities — those are tools, not identities.

| | P1 — The Face-Stealer | P2 — The Telepath |
|---|---|---|
| **Ability** | Steals appearance: mesh + GameplayTags | Copies and pastes consciousness between minds |
| **Range** | Close-range only (must be near the target) | — |
| **Mobility** | Ground-level only — no grapple hook | Grappling hook — rooftop and upper-floor access |
| **Role in stealth** | Social infiltration, bypasses rank-based access | Mind control, removes or redirects individual guards |

They are **hired thieves**, not idealists. The moral weight of their abilities is not their primary concern. The job is.

---

## The Job (Act 1)

A client has hired them to steal an artifact from a privately-owned mansion — grand, eccentric, puzzle-filled (Spencer Mansion in spirit). The mansion is heavily guarded. The client provides entry intelligence but not a safe path. That's on the players.

---

## Lore Delivery Philosophy

> The heroes are young. They don't know this history.
> Players learn it at the same time as the characters — through environmental storytelling: journals, objects, architecture, overheard conversations.
> Never cutscenes. Never exposition dumps.

The clockmaker's apartment in Level 1 is the first lore delivery: players discover the Night of the Purge, the mansion family's fate, and the existence of gifted people — all through a dead man's workshop.
